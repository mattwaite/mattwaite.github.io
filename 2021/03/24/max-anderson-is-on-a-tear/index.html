I’ve got data about the top 150 players by home runs per game. I know the number of games they’ve played and home runs they’ve hit.

homeruns <- read_csv("homerunspergame.csv")

I want to set a minimum number of games that’s higher than what the NCAA uses, so I am going to cut off players who haven’t played 10 games yet.

selected <- homeruns %>% filter(G >= 10)

ma <- homeruns %>% filter(grepl("Max Anderson", Player))

Now I want to see where Max Anderson’s numbers are compared to the top Division I college hitters in home runs per game.

ggplot() + 
  geom_point(data=selected, aes(x=G, y=HR, size=PG), color="grey") +
  geom_point(data=ma, aes(x=G, y=HR, size=PG), color="red") + 
  geom_text_repel(data=ma, aes(x=G, y=HR, label="Max Anderson")) +
  geom_encircle(data=ma, aes(x=G, y=HR, s_shape=.004, expand=.005), colour="red") + 
  theme_minimal() + 
  labs(title="In rarified air already", subtitle="Max Anderson early in the season is in the top 150 college hitters for home runs per game.", caption="Source: NCAA | By Matt Waite")
